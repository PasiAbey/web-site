Sri Lanka is a tropical island situated close to the southern tip of India. The bird life of Sri Lanka is very rich for its size. There are 452 species which are resident, of which 33 are endemic. The other resident species are also found in the nearby Indian mainland, but over 80 have developed distinct Sri Lankan races. Some of these races are very different in their plumage characteristics from the related forms in India.

Endemic Birds

Sri Lanka is home to approximately 34 endemic bird species, a testament to its unique ecological conditions. Notable among them are:

Sri Lanka Junglefowl
The Sri Lanka Junglefowl, the national bird, resembles the red junglefowl but is noted for its vivid colors.
Sri Lanka Blue Magpie
Found in the hill forests, this bird is striking for its bright blue feathers, red bill, and long tail.
Red-faced Malkoha
This forest bird is known for its deep red face, greenish body, and long tail.
Sri Lanka White-eye
Common in the highland forests, this small bird is identified by its characteristic white eye-ring.



Migratory Birds

The island is also a significant stopover for numerous migratory birds, particularly during the northern winter. Some migratory species include:

Asian Openbill
Often seen in wetlands and marshes, feeding on snails.
Eurasian Golden Plover
Visits in smaller numbers to open dry areas.
Barn Swallow
A familiar sight, often observed swooping over fields and open water.



Wetland Birds

The wetlands and coastal regions of Sri Lanka support a diverse array of waterfowl and waders. Important species include:

Painted Stork
Recognizable by its pink tertial feathers and large size.
Black-winged Stilt
Noted for its extremely long legs and contrasting black-and-white plumage.
Greater Flamingo
Occasionally seen in large flocks in lagoons and salt pans.
